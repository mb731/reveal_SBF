Unit 01, Session 4:

Sin Corrupts

Passage: Genesis 6:9-22

Home

Context

  • Sin entered the world with Adam and Eve.
  • Cain killed Abel, continuing the spread of sin.
  • Seth's descendants walked with God, but sin and violence corrupted the rest of humanity.
  • Noah, meaning "rest," was a righteous man who followed God.

Question

When is too much of a good thing a bad thing?

Main Idea: Sin corrupts and must be judged, but God saves those who fear and obey Him.

Sin Corrupts Every Aspect of God's Good Creation

Genesis 6:9-13: "The earth was corrupt in God's sight."

As humans multiplied, sin spread, leading to widespread wickedness.

Question: How do we see this pattern in our world today?

God Judges Sin but Saves Those Who Fear and Obey Him

Genesis 6:14-22: Godly fear always produces obedience in the life of the believer.

Question: How do we live in a world that does not value what God values?

While God permitted people to multiply despite their sinful nature, He preserved a righteous remnant through the line of Seth (Gen. 5). It is also important to note that God examined not only the deeds of humankind but the thoughts and attitudes of their hearts (Gen. 6:5; Heb. 4:12).

God's Judgment

  • God’s judgment was inevitable due to the pervasive corruption.
  • Noah found favor with God.
  • Command to Noah: Build an ark to save his family and two of every kind of animal (Genesis 6:14-22).

The Flood and Covenant

  • God’s Promise: Establishing a covenant with Noah, He preserved Noah's family.
  • Noah's obedience demonstrated his faith.
  • Reflection: How does God’s mercy stand out in this story?

Gospel Connection

The flood represents judgment, but it also prefigures salvation through Jesus.

John 3:16: "Whoever believes in Him shall not perish but have eternal life."

Reflection & Application

  • Head: How can we be "preachers of righteousness" in today’s world?
  • Heart: How does it feel knowing our hearts naturally incline to sin?
  • Hands: How can you encourage someone to walk with God this week?
